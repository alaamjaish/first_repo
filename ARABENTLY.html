Our Method

Why do most methods fail?

Most courses fail because they force you to memorize rules and solve quizzes about them. They treat language like Math.

But research shows that language is acquired, not learned. And acquisition only happens in one way:

Listen + Understand + Repeat with something slightly more complex.

That's it. Your brain picks up the language when you consume lots of messages you actually understand.

But there are three conditions for this to work:

  1. Volume - You need lots of listening.
  2. Comprehension - You must understand 70-90% of what you hear.
  3. Progression - The content needs to gradually become more complex.

How our course uses that?

To speak natural Saudi dialect, your brain needs to be flooded with messages it understands. This course is built on a specific Learning Loop designed to feed your brain exactly what it needs:

STEP 01

Structured Lessons

These are short, focused lessons that decode the patterns of the Saudi dialect.

We don't bore you with abstract grammar. We just present you with sentences that will help you quickly get a pattern of Saudi Arabic.

school
STEP 02

Comprehensible Input

This is the most important part. You will listen to high-volume, relatable content designed specifically for Expats in Riyadh.

It uses the "i+1" Principle: The input is always just above your current level, so you understand the message but naturally absorb new words and grammar without trying.

Why is our Input Different?

Most listening materials are either too fast (native TV) or too boring (children's books).

Our input is relatable to YOUR life. You'll hear stories about offices in Riyadh, ordering from local cafes, and navigating Saudi traffic. Your brain wants to understand it, so it acquires the language faster.

headphones

"But... what about speaking practice?"

I'm glad you asked.

Speaking practice is so important. However, as a beginner, you should spend 80% of your time listening and comprehending, and only 20% speaking. If you try to speak without enough input, you're just translating poorly from English in your head.

Does this course include speaking sessions?

This offer focuses on the core 80%--giving you the massive input you need. It does not include live sessions.

However, when you're ready to speak, we'll share resources and recommendations to help you set up the other 20%.

Will this course make me fluent? expand_more

This is a foundational course. This course will not make you fully fluent, but it will give you a strong foundation in Saudi Arabic.

Inside the course, you will learn almost all the essential grammar rules you need to speak Arabic in daily life.

By the end of the course, you'll be able to:

  • Introduce yourself and talk about your background
  • Handle common daily situations (work, cafés, shops, directions, etc.)
  • Have simple conversations with Saudis
  • Understand basic spoken Arabic in clear, everyday contexts

After this Foundational Course, there will be two follow-up programs -- an Intermediate course and an Advanced course -- for learners who want to keep going and reach higher levels of fluency. Fluency comes with more time and more input.

Do I need to know how to read Arabic, or can I learn without reading? expand_more

You do not need to read Arabic to start this course.

Inside this Foundational Course, all Arabic is written fully in Arabic script.

During the lessons, I read every Arabic sentence out loud, so you can rely on listening only and still follow the course, even if you don't read.

However, skipping reading will usually make your progress slower in the mid and long term.

Learning the script is a small, one-time investment (about 10-20 hours for most learners) that unlocks a lot of extra potential: you'll be able to read signs, menus, messages, and every line of Arabic in the course much more easily -- and your overall progress will be smoother.

You can watch this [free 3-hour YouTube reading course] to learn the Arabic script if you want to!

What if I already know some Arabic or have studied Standard Arabic (Fus`ha)? expand_more

If you already know some Arabic, you will probably move faster.

  • If you know a few phrases or have taken a course before, this program will organize what you know, fill in gaps, and give you a clear structure.
  • If you studied Fus`ha (Standard Arabic), this course will show you the Najdi dialect people actually speak in Riyadh, so you can finally follow real conversations.
  • If you've already covered all the beginner topics of the Saudi dialect (you know all the tenses, essential grammar rules, and the most common 500 words) then this course might not be the right level for you.
Which dialect does this course teach, and will I be understood in other Saudi cities Gulf countries? expand_more

The course teaches Najdi Arabic -- the dialect spoken in Riyadh and central Saudi Arabia. It is widely understood across the country and is often used in professional settings.

If you live in places like Jeddah, the UAE, Qatar, or Kuwait, people will still understand you to a large extent. Gulf dialects share a lot of vocabulary and structure, so the foundation you build here will transfer well, even though each city has its own flavor.
